Project Boondock

A Mountain Engineering Center towards Sustainable Infrastructure and Upland Water Security is a program that aims to connect different natural systems and human activities in the mountains in a sustainable mountain development context. The aforementioned program has two major components interconnected in a sustainable mountain system which are: the upland water security, and the infrastructure or engineering intervention. The upland water security component includes the upland river and spring water assessment and management, and the artificial ground water recharge. On the other hand, the infrastructure and engineering intervention includes slope protection analysis and management of terraces and road infrastructure, and cable supported water and transport infrastructures.

Ensuring Water Security and Climate-Resilience for Mountain Indigenous Communities of the Cordillera through Enhanced Decision Support Tools

The core research funded by National Research Council of the Philippines (NRCP) is titled, “Ensuring Water Security and Climate-Resilience for Mountain Indigenous Communities of the Cordillera through Enhanced Decision Support Tools.” Its base goal is to ensure water security for mountain communities in CAR or through technology-enhanced decision support tools for the management of safe water supplies, watersheds, and river quality.
